About me

I received the B.S. with Highest Honors in Applied Science and Engineering at UNC Chapel Hill in 2013. While at UNC, I was an undergraduate researcher in the Optical Coherence Imaging Lab. I then spent a year as an Associate in Research in the DISP Lab at Duke University. Since 2014, I have been a graduate student in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at UC Berkeley, where I work with Associate Prof. Laura Waller, where I explore new and novel ways of designing imaging hardware and reconstruction algorithms together, optimizing for performance, robustness, and price.
